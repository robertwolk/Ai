# Style Library

Core directions for accent walls and room design. Adapt palettes to the user's fixed furniture; hex values are starting points, not mandates. Costs are Long Island contractor ballparks (2026), materials unless noted.

## Pairing rules (apply before choosing a direction)

- **Grey / greige wood furniture** → pair with light white oak or ash for airy rooms; walnut/brass for moody-luxe. Never match grey-on-grey wall wood to a grey bed — the furniture disappears.
- **One statement per small room.** Under ~160 sq ft, a single feature wall. If the user has a paint accent AND a wood treatment planned, cut one.
- **Vertical elements raise ceilings.** 8' ceilings + vertical slats or full-height battens; horizontal treatments (shiplap) only with 9'+ ceilings.
- **Chair rails and wallpaper** in a room getting a feature wall: recommend removal everywhere, not just the feature wall — half-treatments read as unfinished.
- **Sconce wiring** goes in before any wall treatment. Raise it in every design that includes panels or slats.

## Directions

### 1. Scandinavian Airy (light & airy briefs)
- Treatment: white oak or ash vertical slat panels (acoustic felt-backed prefab), full height, full wall width
- Palette: White Oak #DCC9A8 · Warm White walls #F4F1E9 · Oatmeal linen #EFEAE0 · Matte black accents #2E2B27
- Cost: ~$22–30/sq ft installed panel area; 100 sq ft wall ≈ $2,400–3,200 materials
- Trade-off: highest material cost of the light options; prefab quality varies — specify real-wood veneer, not printed MDF

### 2. Boutique Hotel Warm (RH-gallery look)
- Treatment: walnut slat panels, brass or aged-bronze sconces, crisp white bedding
- Palette: Walnut #8A6748 · Warm White #F1EDE4 · Aged Brass #A88958 · Camel #C7B7A0
- Cost: ~$26–36/sq ft panel area
- Trade-off: visually shrinks rooms under ~160 sq ft; best at 9'+ ceilings or with abundant natural light

### 3. Classic Hamptons / Board & Batten (budget architectural)
- Treatment: painted 1×3 batten grid over skim-coated wall, tone one shade deeper than field walls
- Palette: Greige #E9E4D8 · Winter White #F4F1E9 · Coastal Blue accent #B9C2C6 · White trim
- Cost: $600–1,200 materials for a typical wall; strong DIY candidate
- Trade-off: less texture/warmth than wood; quiet rather than statement

### 4. Moody Modern (drama briefs)
- Treatment: full-height flat panels with recessed reveals, saturated paint (navy, charcoal, deep green)
- Palette: Midnight Blue #2E3A47 · Warm White #F1EEE6 · Brass #B49A64 · Light bedding for contrast
- Cost: $900–1,600 materials
- Trade-off: opposite of airy; commits the room to evening moodiness — needs strong layered lighting

### 5. Staging Neutral (real estate listings, senior transitions)
- Treatment: paint only — warm white or greige throughout, no permanent treatments
- Palette: Greige #E5E0D5 · Soft White #F6F4EE · Warm Wood tones · One plant-green accent
- Cost: paint + labor only; furniture rental separate
- Rules: broad-appeal over personality; maximize perceived size and light; for senior spaces add lighting layers, contrast edges on steps, zero trip hazards

## Cost model (Nassau/Suffolk, 2026, moderate confidence)

- Wallpaper strip + skim coat: $2–5/sq ft — THE wildcard; old paper on plaster can double it
- Interior paint, walls+trim+ceiling, one room: $500–1,000 labor
- Slat panel install labor: $8–15/sq ft
- Sconce rough-in + fixture install (pair, existing circuit nearby): $400–800
- Always present ranges, split materials vs. labor, and name the wildcard.
